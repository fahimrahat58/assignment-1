Great choice. FAQ + Newsletter is a strong combination because they serve different purposes:

FAQ removes hesitation for users who are almost ready to register.
Newsletter captures users who aren't ready to register yet, so you don't lose them.

Here's how I'd approach it as a UI/UX designer.

Step 1: Decide the Page Order

Your current flow is approximately:

Hero
→ About/Stats
→ Speakers
→ Pricing
→ Venue
→ Footer

The improved flow should be:

Hero
→ About
→ Speakers
→ Pricing
→ FAQ
→ Newsletter
→ Footer

Why?

After users see the price, they naturally have questions.

Once those questions are answered, give them one final action:

Buy a ticket
or
Subscribe for updates

This creates a smooth decision-making flow.

Step 2: Design the FAQ Section

Think of the FAQ as a calm, simple section—not something visually heavy.

Section Header

Start with:

Frequently Asked Questions

Then a short supporting sentence like:

Everything you need to know before attending.

Avoid long paragraphs.

Step 3: Decide the Layout

For a modern conference website, there are three common layouts:

Option 1 (Recommended)

Accordion list

▼ What is included in the ticket?

▼ Can I get a refund?

▼ Will sessions be recorded?

▼ Is parking available?

▼ Are meals included?

Only one answer expands at a time.

This keeps the page compact and easy to scan.

Option 2

Two-column FAQ grid

Good for desktops but less ideal if answers are long.

Option 3

Cards

Not recommended here because it takes too much vertical space.

Step 4: Keep the Questions Short

People scan first.

Aim for questions with about 5–10 words, for example:

Who should attend?
What's included in my ticket?
Can I transfer my ticket?
Will sessions be recorded?
Is parking available?
Are meals included?
What should I bring?
Can students attend?
Step 5: Keep Answers Concise

Each answer should be around 2–4 lines.

Visitors shouldn't have to read long paragraphs.

Step 6: Add a Final CTA Below the FAQ

Once users finish reading:

Still have questions?

A button such as:

Contact Us

This gives users a clear next step if their question isn't answered.

Step 7: Newsletter Section

This should feel different from the FAQ.

It acts as the final "soft" conversion before the footer.

Headline

Something simple like:

Stay Updated

or

Don't Miss Conference Updates

Supporting Text

One short sentence.

For example:

Get speaker announcements, schedule updates, and early-bird ticket offers delivered to your inbox.

Step 8: Layout

Keep it minimal.

A centered layout works best:

Headline

Short description

[ Email Input ]

[ Subscribe Button ]

Avoid adding extra fields like name or company. One email field is enough.

Step 9: Visual Design

Give the newsletter section a subtle visual distinction from the rest of the page.

For example:

Slightly darker background if the page is light.
Slightly lighter background if the page is dark.
Rounded corners.
More generous padding than surrounding sections.

This helps it stand out without feeling disconnected.

Step 10: End with the Footer

After the newsletter, transition naturally into the footer.

The footer should include links like:

About
Speakers
Schedule
Venue
Contact
Social Media
Copyright
Final Flow
Hero

↓

Conference Highlights

↓

Speakers

↓

Pricing

↓

FAQ

↓

Newsletter

↓

Footer

This order aligns well with how users make decisions: they first understand the event, then evaluate its value, resolve any remaining questions, and finally either register or subscribe for future updates. It keeps the experience focused and conversion-oriented.
